- Album sales are
decreasing.

Over the years we have seen a decrease in album sales, almost two/thirds of what we used to be able to sell.

Comparison between then and now.

Then

Michael Jackson, Thriller - 1982
with 68 million album sales world wide, making it the best selling album of all time.

68 million

Now

Adele, 21 - 2011
with 28 million albums sold. That's 2.5 times less than Michael Jackson's "Thriller".

28 million

Although "Adele" sold over 28 million albums in 2011, she is the only one in our current decade who has been able to sell that many copies. The massive hit band "One Direction" only managed to sell 4 million copies in 2013. Only three songs managed to pass the 5 million sales mark in 2013. Even the album from the hit movie in 2014: "Frozen", only sold 10 million copies world wide.

In 2014, the total amount of albums sold were 257 million. Only 140.8 million of those were CD's.

SPOTIFY = MONEY?

Well. The easiest way to understand how it works, is to imagine that you yourself are an artist or have a band.

Spotify payments varies from 0.048 to 0.064kr per streamed song. That means that if you had

1.000.000 plays

on one of your songs (which takes a major breakthrough) you’d only get

64.000kr in total.

Then, Spotify will take 30% of that (19.200kr), and you’re left with

44.800kr.

If you had a producer helping you on the song, she/he would typically take 3%, leaving you with

42.880kr.

If you have a manager that helped you finance your project, she/he will usually take 20%, leaving you with

30.080kr.

If you have a record label you need to split the money with them. The amount varies depending on your contract, but it’s usually around 15 and 20%, leaving you with an estimated

17.280kr - 20.480kr

if you are a solo artist.

If you are a band however, the money is usually split between band members. If you were a band of 4, and all got an equal share you’d end up with

4320kr - 5120kr.

- Musicians are left with
less money
for their music.

It's not just your band that's having a hard time. Your average famous artist is struggling as well. There is so little money in album sales nowadays, the record/booking labels often have to up the prices on concert tickets to be able to go around. Most bands nowadays make more money from playing shows and selling merchandise. (t-shirts, hoodies etc.)
